MyHand-RLX

The MyHand-RLX incorporates small scale vibration motors into the MyHand Robot. I developed a modular mounting system for the vibration motors centralizing and strengthening electrical connections. This decreased robot don and doff times during patient testing session and ensured connections were robust enough to endure testing procedures and transportation.

The MyHand Robot

Excessive vibration in precision equipment can lead to mechanical wear, signal noise, and reduced performance. This project focused on developing a compact, modular vibration isolation mount for sensitive devices. The goal was to design a solution that is lightweight, easily integrated, and capable of damping low-frequency vibrations.

Design Exploration

Initial sketch concepts focused on elastomer-based damping and geometric flexibility.

Rapid prototyping was used to evaluate spring-damper pair configurations and compression characteristics.

CAD & Simulation

CAD design of the mount using SolidWorks with dynamic simulation to test modal frequencies and deformation profiles under load.

Fabrication & Testing

Testing was conducted with accelerometer feedback to validate performance across a range of frequencies and weights.

Results & Reflections

The final mount reduced transmitted vibration by over 60% in the target frequency range (10–80 Hz). Its modularity allows adaptation to various devices, and future iterations may include tunable damping using magnetorheological fluid or smart elastomers.

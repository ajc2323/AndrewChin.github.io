Compost Smart

Overview

Compost Smart is a modular smart compost bin monitoring system built for community gardens. It provides real-time feedback on temperature, moisture, and pH, allowing better compost management and education. I led hardware development and systems integration.

Problem Identification

Urban gardens often lack tools to monitor and manage compost conditions effectively. Without visibility into pH, moisture, or temperature, composting can become inefficient, odorous, or pest-prone. This project sought to improve that experience through embedded sensing and low-cost electronics.

Design & Prototyping

We built a low-fidelity Arduino-based monitoring unit using analog pH, temperature, and moisture sensors. Data was displayed on an LCD screen and optionally transmitted via Bluetooth for mobile logging. The design focused on modularity, weather resistance, and user-friendly operation.

Impact & Learnings

The prototype helped community gardens monitor compost conditions with ease. Gardeners reported improved compost consistency and reduced odor. Future iterations will explore CO₂ sensing and long-range data logging using LoRa. This project highlighted the value of hardware design for urban sustainability and education.

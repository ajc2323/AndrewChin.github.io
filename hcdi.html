Compost Smart: Smart Compost Monitor

Compost Smart is a modular smart compost bin monitoring system built for community gardens. It provides real-time feedback on temperature, moisture, and pH, allowing better compost management and education. I worked as the lead hardware developer and systems integrator.

Problem Identification

Urban gardens often lack the tools to manage compost effectively. Users may not know if their compost is too dry, too acidic, or too cold. Without this data, compost can take too long to break down or produce odors and pests.

Research & Opportunity

Through user interviews and observational research in NYC community gardens, we discovered a clear demand for more accessible compost monitoring tools. Our survey data showed most gardeners wanted digital insights but had limited technical expertise.

Concept & Prototyping

We designed a sensor hub using Arduino and low-cost analog sensors (moisture, temperature, and pH). The data is displayed on a local LCD and optionally transmitted via Bluetooth.

Outcomes & Reflections

The final prototype allowed users to understand their compost pile condition with just a glance. We observed higher user engagement and fewer compost issues in monitored bins. The modular nature of the system allows for future add-ons like CO₂ sensors or LoRa transmission for remote sites.
